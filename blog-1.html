What is Final Expense Insurance and its Types?

Leave a Comment / By Marketing / October 17, 2023

Final expense insurance is a type of life insurance policy designed to cover the expenses associated with a person’s funeral. It is specifically intended to ease the financial burden on the family during this challenging time.

Final expense insurance policies are smaller in amount compared to traditional life insurance policies. They typically range from $5,000 to $25,000, although some policies may offer higher coverage amounts. This policy is offered to individuals who are concerned about their funeral and related expenses and wish to provide for their loved ones in terms of end-of-life costs.

Types of Final Expense Policies

Simplified Issue

This type of policy does not require a medical exam for approval. Applicants are asked a series of health-related questions on the application form. The underwriting process is simplified, and coverage is often granted more quickly compared to traditional life insurance policies. However, the premium rates for simplified issue policies may be higher due to the increased risk associated with the absence of a medical examination.

Guaranteed Issue

This policy is designed for individuals who may have difficulty obtaining coverage due to pre-existing medical conditions. Guaranteed issue policies do not require a medical exam and have no health-related questions on the application. As a result, acceptance is guaranteed for anyone who meets the age requirements, typically between 50 and 85 years of age. However, the coverage amount for guaranteed issue policies is generally lower, and the premiums are usually higher compared to other types of life insurance.

Grace Period

It is important to note that final expense insurance policies may have a classified death benefit provision. This means that if the insured passes away within a certain period after the policy is issued (usually the first two years), the full death benefit may not be payable. The beneficiary may receive a partial amount of the premium paid. After the classified period of two years, the full death benefit is payable regardless of the cause of death.

The funds from a final expense insurance policy can be used for various end-of-life expenses. These may include costs related to the funeral, burial, cremation, purchasing a casket, securing a cemetery plot, obtaining a headstone, organizing memorial services, and settling any outstanding medical bills. The policyholder can usually designate one or more beneficiaries to receive the death benefit after their passing.

Make a Better Decision

It is crucial to review the terms and conditions, as well as the coverage limitations of any final expense insurance policy. Comparing different policies from reputable insurance providers can be helpful. Additionally, seeking guidance from an expert can assist individuals in making a more informed decision based on their specific needs.
